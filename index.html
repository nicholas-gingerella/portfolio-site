My Drive

Mission Statement

Working as a software engineer on various software products and teams has taught me important lessons that shape how I go about my work, and what values I feel are most important to a practitioner in this field.

Be Inquisitive
Ask questions and analyze the root cause of problems when searching for solutions. Break down complex issues and create elegant solutions that provide lasting value, rather than short lived patches.

Value Quality
Have an eye for detail and usability when designing software. Your goal is to create a quality product which presents an enjoyable user experience to your target audience.

Never Stop Learning
Learn from your mistakes and those around you, always seeking knowledge and tools to boost your productivity and expand your skills. True learning requires effort, don't always take the easy path.

Be A Collaborator
Work with others to take on the big challenges you can't do alone. Through the teamwork of many contributing to a shared vision, we can all do great things.

Python

Team Building & Collaboration

I'm an advocate for fostering a healthy team culture. Teams where members feel comfortable and respect one another leads to open collaboration where everyone is able to improve and contribute to a shared goal.

University of California Riverside

Python Programming Teaching Assistant
February 2014 - May 2014

Responsibilities:

  • Lead programming lessons and exercise sessions
  • Mentor students individually in areas that they struggled with
  • Create tests and quizzes to assess understanding of covered material

During my time as a student at the University of California Riverside, I took on a position as an assistant instructor for Python programming class for 9th grade students at a local middle school sponsored by the university.

My primary role was to teach the class the fundamentals for programming using Python through a series of classes held throughout the quarter.

Communicating technical concepts to children who had never programmed was quite the challenge, and I learned a lot about communication and fostering a comfortable learning environment for these newcomers.

But even with all the hurdles faced, that class performed beautifully, and by the end of the quarter, they were all armed with a new tool at their disposal and a new found appreciation for the applications they use every day.

Full-Stack Developer
